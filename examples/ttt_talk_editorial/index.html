The baseline Best-of-N

The simplest way to search.

Sample many solutions from a frozen model, then keep the best one. That's Best-of-N.

THE CATCH every sample comes from the same frozen model. It never learns between attempts.
More compute means more guesses, never a better guesser 05
The intuition How we solve hard problems

No one solves a hard problem on the first try.

We try, get stuck, see what failed, and come back sharper.
Test-time training gives the model this same loop 06
The catch What RL optimizes

The catch: RL hill-climbs toward the average, not the best.

RL maximizes the average reward. Discovery keeps only the best sample, so the max is what counts. 07
The idea, in one line TTT-Discover

Reinforcement learning at test time,
on a single problem.
Optimize for the best result, not the average.

The policy is disposable. We don't need a model that generalizes, just one artifact that beats the state of the art.

Train on the problem · keep the best solution · throw the weights away 08

The equations · intuition Why eβR chases the best

Turn up β, and the weight piles onto the best rollout.

w(y) = eβR(y) / Σ eβR, a soft-max over rewards. As β → ∞ it tracks the max, not the mean. 10
